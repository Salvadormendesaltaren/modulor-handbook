> "Our future practice"

### What We're Building

### Construction Areas

| Area | Focus |
|---|---|
| **Presence** | Visibility and positioning |
| **Capabilities** | Skills and tools |
| **Practice** | Methodology and processes |
| **Culture** | Values and team dynamics |

### Model 226 - How It Works

Business innovation-oriented relationship model that combines strategic vision, business architecture and functions as an experience pilot.

**Phase I — Strategy** (1 week to 1 quarter)
- Definition of vision, opportunities and scenarios
- Business architecture and roadmap design

**Phase II — Implementation**
- Resources are allocated and the defined plan is executed
- A project director leads and coordinates
- Squads of 3-4 people (design, development, marketing, etc.) working by objectives

### Lab 226 with Tailor Hub

Together with **Tailor Hub**, we design and activate innovation programs that connect strategic vision, business design and technology:

- **Co-creation labs:** Align perspectives, identify opportunities, actionable roadmaps
- **Technical audits:** Architecture, data and systems evaluation for AI-driven transformation

## 07. Ideal Client Profile (ICP)

### Company Profile

- **Size:** Corporations and startups with tech product
- **Sectors:** Tech, SaaS, Finance, Energy, Retail, Health
- **Geography:** Primarily Spain, selective international projects
- **Digital Maturity:** Medium-high
- **Minimum Budget:** >EUR30k per project

### Decision Maker Profile

- **Typical Role:** CPO, Head of Product, CEO (startups), Innovation Director
- **Motivations:** Differentiate, professionalize product, scale
- **Frustrations:** Overwhelmed internal teams, lack of strategic vision

### Purchase Triggers

- New digital product launch
- Existing product redesign
- Design system creation
- Rebranding or new brand
- Funding round (startups)
- Digital transformation (corporations)

### Good Fit Signals

**Green flags:**
- Value quality over extreme speed
- Have adequate budget (>EUR30k)
- Direct access to decision makers
- Understand the value of design

**Red flags:**
- Urgencies without budget
- Multiple approval layers
- Only looking for "hands" not strategy
- Comparison with cheap freelancers
